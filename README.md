## üß© Problem 1: Manufacturing Profit Maximization

### Business Scenario
A manufacturing company produces two products:
- Product A
- Product B

Each product consumes machine hours and labor hours, which are limited.
The goal is to determine the optimal production quantities that **maximize total profit**.

### Optimization Type
- **Objective:** Maximize profit
- **Technique:** Linear Programming
- **Decision Variables:** Units of Product A and Product B
- **Constraints:** Machine hours, labor hours, non-negativity

---

## üì¶ Problem 2: Supply Chain Optimization

### Business Scenario
A company operates:
- 2 Warehouses
- 3 Retail Stores

Each warehouse has limited supply capacity, and each store has fixed demand.
Transportation costs vary between warehouse‚Äìstore pairs.

### Optimization Type
- **Objective:** Minimize transportation cost
- **Technique:** Linear Programming
- **Decision Variables:** Units shipped from each warehouse to each store
- **Constraints:** Supply limits, demand fulfillment
